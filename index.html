§ The mandate

The cloud is not cheaper.
And it’s off‑limits.

Per-token prices look tiny until you add them up: per seat, per month, forever — at rates someone else raises once you depend on them. Over its life, owning the machine beats renting intelligence by the drip. And even if it didn’t: your files aren’t allowed out anyway.

Honest math

Hardware amortizes in four years, then keeps working — subscriptions never stop. Measured against staff time (€60–80k a year per routine full-time role), the system pays for itself in year one. We’ll show you the spreadsheet.

§203

StGB — professional secrecy

Lawyers, tax advisors and doctors face criminal liability for exposing client secrets. Handing files to a foreign-controlled cloud is a risk no engagement letter can wave away.

Art.9

GDPR — special-category data

Health records and legal matters carry the strictest processing rules in European law. The safest processor is the one that never receives the data.

CLOUD

US CLOUD Act — foreign reach

US providers can be compelled to hand over data held anywhere — a Frankfurt data center included. Server location doesn’t end the exposure. Ownership does.

T&C The fine print

You wouldn’t sign
these terms anywhere else.

Set the law aside for a moment and read the deal itself. Every cloud AI subscription carries the same six clauses — most firms notice them only once they depend on the service.

Clause 01Jurisdiction

The switch sits in America

The strongest cloud models are run by American companies under American law. Whatever you build on them inherits that jurisdiction — and its politics.

Clause 02Termination

Access can end overnight

Export rules tighten, policies shift, accounts get flagged by automated systems. Every cloud contract reserves the right to suspend service at the provider’s discretion. Your workflow runs at their pleasure.

Clause 03Custody

Your files become attack surface

Everything you send is transmitted, logged and “retained for abuse monitoring” on infrastructure you will never audit. Breaches happen to the biggest names in the business. Data that never leaves can’t leak in transit.

Clause 04Pricing

Today’s prices are a promotion

Token prices are subsidized by venture capital to win market share. Every subsidized platform in history raised prices once its customers were locked in. Budget for the price after the promotion.

Clause 05Perpetuity

The rent never ends

A subscription compounds forever and buys you nothing. Hardware amortizes in four years — then keeps working. Only one of these ends up on your balance sheet as an asset.

Clause 06Control

The model changes under you

Cloud models get deprecated, swapped and silently retuned. The model you validated in January may answer differently in June. On your own hardware, nothing changes until you decide it does.

The short version

Owning is sexy. Renting is dependence with a monthly invoice.

CO₂ The footprint

Cloud AI isn’t
climate-friendly.

Hyperscale AI runs on whatever power is fastest to build. The EU is heading the other way: under European sustainability reporting and the German Energy Efficiency Act, an untracked AI footprint is about to become a line item you pay for. A rack in your own building is a footprint you control — and can make sustainable, step by step.

40°C

Winter heat, reused

A working rack is a 3–5 kW heater. In winter its waste heat feeds your building’s heating loop instead of a cooling tower — exactly the heat reuse German data-center law now rewards.

kWp

Powered by your roof

Pair the system with solar panels: daytime inference runs on your own generation, the night batch draws cheap off-peak power. Your energy mix is your choice — the cloud’s isn’t.

CO₂e

Metered, reported, yours

One machine, one meter: you can state your AI footprint to the kilowatt-hour in any ESG or CSRD report. Try getting that number out of a hyperscaler.
